SCRUM

Scrum is a software development methodology. It is a set of rules or guidelines that are followed during the software engineering cycle. It is a form of agile method of software development, that is useful for faster development of the software by making the process of adapting to the changing requirements of the end-user easier and efficient.

The basic principle of scrum is to develop the software incrementally. The development of a software is divided into a set of small tasks and a set of tasks is selected to be developed over a small period of time. The final product requirements are provided by the product owner while the small set of tasks to be implemented will be selected by a person in the development team.

Terminologies involved

There are various terminologies that are used in the scrum. The names may vary in usage but the core concept or principle remains same with slight changes.Some of the key terms that are involved are as follows:

Product Owner

Product owner is the one who wants a particular software to be built for a group of customers. The product owner is responsible for coming up with the requirements that has to be met, and also interact with the customers to identify any problems with the current version of the software and any new requirements that are not met in the current version of the software.

Product Backlog

Product backlog contains all the requirements of the product that are identified by the product owner. Product owner manages the product backlog.

Sprint Cycle

The software is developed over small period of cycles which varies from a couple of days to oen-two weeks. At the begging of the cycle, a set of tasks are chosen to be implemented. These tasks will be implemented during the current cycle. This allows the product owner to modify the requirements for the next cycle.

Sprint Backlog

Sprint backlog is the set of requirements that are being implemented in the ongoing sprint cycle. These requirements are derived from the product backlog. The sprint backlog changes for each sprint cycle.

Scrum Leader

Scrum leader is responsible for selecting the tasks from the product backlog that will be part of each sprint cycle. Scrum leader interacts with product owner and then selects the tasks that are of higher priority from the product backlog and add them to the sprint cycle.
